U.S.-Israel Strikes on Iran Send Oil Markets Into Shock, Hormuz Disruption Risk Priced In
Joint U.S.-Israeli strikes killed Iranian Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei over the weekend, sending Brent crude surging more than 10% to around $80 a barrel in early over-the-counter trading, with analysts warning prices could reach $100 if Strait of Hormuz shipments are disrupted. The Strait handles a significant share of global oil and gas flows, making any sustained military activity in the Persian Gulf a systemic energy shock. Traders are watching OPEC+ responses and attack patterns closely; inflationary pressure on fuel prices is already materializing.

Iran Attacks Dubai, Threatening Its Safe-Haven Status for Global Wealth
Iran launched retaliatory missiles and drones targeting Israel and several Persian Gulf states, including countries hosting U.S. military bases — with Dubai caught in the blast radius of geopolitical risk it has spent decades marketing its way around. The city-state's identity as a tax-free, stable hub for global elite capital is now visibly cracked, with Fortune describing potential fallout as 'catastrophic.' For the estimated trillions in assets parked there by Russian oligarchs, Gulf sovereigns, and international family offices, the calculus on safe-haven geography just changed.

Polymarket Insiders Reportedly Netted $1.5M Betting on Exact Date of Iran Strikes
Six accounts flagged as 'suspected insiders' on Polymarket earned an estimated $1.5 million wagering on the precise timing of U.S. strikes against Iran, with total Iran-related betting volume hitting $529 million on the platform according to Bloomberg. The pattern of winning trades — opened shortly before the strikes were announced — has drawn immediate scrutiny over who had foreknowledge and how prediction markets interact with classified geopolitical intelligence. This lands alongside the Giannis-Kalshi controversy and raises a broader structural question: prediction markets are now large enough that insider trading in them constitutes a serious market integrity problem.

Trump Says Iran Conflict Will Last 'Four Weeks or So,' Leaves Door Open to Talks
President Trump told the Daily Mail he expects the military campaign against Iran to run approximately four weeks, framing it as a calibrated timeline while acknowledging the country's size. He signalled openness to Iranian negotiations but offered no indication talks were imminent, saying Tehran 'should have talked last week, not this week.' Markets are effectively being handed a rough duration estimate from the commander-in-chief, which is either reassuring or alarming depending on what happens in week two.

New York Post Markets
Berkshire Hathaway Q4 Operating Profit Drops 30% as Abel Takes the Wheel
Berkshire Hathaway reported a 30% decline in Q4 operating profit to $10.2 billion, driven by a 38% collapse in insurance earnings and falling interest income on its $373.3 billion cash pile as rates came down. The conglomerate also took a $4.5 billion writedown on its 26.9% stake in Occidental Petroleum, marking the second such writedown in 2025 after concluding the stock's decline was not temporary. New CEO Greg Abel, in his first shareholder letter, acknowledged that some businesses need better operating performance — a pointed admission that the Buffett era's hands-off style has its limits.

USA Today / FTW Politics
Iran May Withdraw from 2026 World Cup After U.S.-Israeli Strikes Kill Khamenei
Iran's soccer federation president Mehdi Taj told Iranian sports outlet Varzesh3 that participation in the 2026 FIFA World Cup — hosted across the U.S., Canada, and Mexico — is now in serious doubt following the strikes, saying the team cannot 'look forward to the World Cup with hope.' FIFA said it is 'monitoring the developments' and has not clarified a replacement process, though its rulebook points to the UAE as the most likely substitute as the highest-ranked non-qualifier from the Asian Football Confederation. The geopolitical irony of Iran potentially being replaced by the UAE — whose safe-haven status Iran just attacked — is not lost on anyone.

OpenAI Hid Knowledge of Tumbler Ridge Shooter's ChatGPT Chats While Lobbying Ottawa for $2B AI Contract
An op-ed by Harvard-affiliated data scientists Nathan Sanders and Bruce Schneier reveals that OpenAI flagged ChatGPT interactions by the Tumbler Ridge, B.C., shooter — including gun-violence discussions — but internally blocked employees from alerting law enforcement, and said nothing when its lobbyist met with the B.C. government afterward. The revelation arrives as Canada's Carney administration deploys $2 billion over five years in its Sovereign AI Compute Strategy, with OpenAI aggressively pursuing a share through its 'OpenAI for Countries' initiative described as operating 'in coordination with the U.S. government.' The authors argue Canada should build wholly public AI infrastructure rather than funnel sovereign capital to a company that has now demonstrated it cannot be trusted with basic transparency.

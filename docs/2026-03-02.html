Modi Chairs Emergency Security Meeting as U.S.-Israel Strikes Kill Khamenei, Shut Hormuz
Prime Minister Modi convened India's Cabinet Committee on Security after U.S. and Israeli strikes on Iran killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and triggered closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical artery for Indian oil imports. With roughly nine million Indian nationals across the Gulf and West Asia, and airspace across the region effectively shut, New Delhi is managing both an energy supply crisis and a potential mass evacuation scenario. The meeting was attended by Defence, Home, External Affairs, and Finance ministers, alongside NSA Ajit Doval and RBI veteran Shaktikanta Das.

Oil Markets Enter Iran Conflict With Minimal Buffer; Brent Could Hit $80
Oil prices rose more than 2% following the U.S.-Israel strikes on Iran, with Barclays flagging Brent crude could reach $80 a barrel on sustained tension. The market's core vulnerability is structural: spare capacity is thin, Iranian supply is now at risk, and the Strait of Hormuz — through which roughly 20% of global oil trade passes — has been closed by Tehran. Any prolonged disruption compounds an already tight inventory picture.

Thousands Stranded as Middle East Airspace Closes; Dubai Airport Damaged
Iran, Iraq, Israel, Syria, Kuwait, and the UAE closed their airspace following Saturday's strikes, leaving thousands of international travelers — including hundreds of Australians — stranded in Doha, Dubai, and Abu Dhabi. Dubai International Airport, which handles approximately 300,000 passengers daily and serves as a hub for Emirates, sustained damage, as did Abu Dhabi's Zayed International, used by Etihad. The Australian Travel Industry Association warned travelers against panic-canceling flights, noting they would forfeit consumer protections by doing so.

Saudi Aramco reroutes crude as Hormuz closure sends Brent near $120/bbl
Saudi Aramco is diverting oil shipments to the Red Sea port of Yanbu after the Strait of Hormuz was effectively closed by the U.S.-Israel campaign against Iran, with Brent crude futures surging to near $120 per barrel. Multiple Middle Eastern producers are curtailing output amid the disruption. Analysts warn that elevated fuel prices could persist for months, with transport and food costs set to follow crude higher.

New York Post Politics
Even Lindsey Graham urges Israel to show caution after strikes on Iran fuel depots
Sen. Lindsey Graham (R-SC), one of the most hawkish voices in Washington on Iran, publicly called on Israel to be more selective about its target selection after Saturday strikes hit 30 fuel storage sites and other energy infrastructure in Tehran, sparking toxic fires and acidic rain. The Trump administration, which received advance notice of the strikes, was reportedly caught off guard by their scale, with officials warning the attacks could push Iranians to rally behind the regime rather than against it. Graham simultaneously told Fox News that Operation Epic Fury would escalate further within two weeks.

Free Republic / Ron Paul Institute Macro
Ron Paul warns Iran war cost — $891M/day and rising — threatens dollar reserve status
The U.S. government is spending approximately $891.4 million per day on the Iran war according to CSIS estimates, with a $50 billion supplemental funding request expected to land in Congress shortly — a figure Paul calls a floor, not a ceiling. He argues the fiscal pressure will force the Federal Reserve to monetize debt at scale, accelerating foreign central bank diversification away from dollar-denominated assets. Paul warns a resulting loss of reserve currency status could trigger an economic contraction worse than the Great Depression.

French President Emmanuel Macron confirmed the deployment of the aircraft carrier Charles de Gaulle and its battle group near Cyprus following drone and missile strikes on the island, declaring that "when someone attacks Cyprus, they attack all of Europe." Economic Times Markets
NSE moves toward long-delayed $2.5 billion IPO, merchant banker appointments due in March
India's National Stock Exchange is set to appoint merchant bankers this month for a potential $2.5 billion offer-for-sale, reviving a listing process that has been stalled for years by regulatory delays and governance concerns. The IPO would rank among the largest in Indian market history and would give public investors direct exposure to one of Asia's highest-volume exchange operators. Regulatory clearance from SEBI remains the outstanding variable.

GlobeNewswire / Savage & Symbio Energy
First hydrogen fuel cell Class-8 drayage truck commissioned at 17,000 lb curb weight
Savage Companies and Symbio North America have commissioned what they describe as the world's lightest zero-emission Class-8 tractor, a 4x2 hydrogen fuel cell electric drayage truck weighing approximately 17,000 pounds with full-shift range and no mid-shift refueling requirement. The vehicle uses a 150kW StackPack architecture paired with 34kg of onboard hydrogen storage at 70 MPa. Savage intends to use the platform as the basis for transitioning its 70-plus California drayage fleet to zero-emission operations.

Philippines declares national energy emergency as Brent crude tops $100 on Hormuz blockade
President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. signed an executive order declaring a national energy emergency, citing 'imminent danger' to the country's energy supply stemming from the blockade of the Strait of Hormuz — through which 20% of the world's oil and liquefied natural gas flows in peacetime. May delivery Brent crude topped $100 a barrel again on Tuesday, while Iran warned it is preparing to fully close the strait and strike infrastructure, though it allowed a Thai-flagged vessel to pass as a gesture of diplomatic selectivity. Trump, for his part, postponed strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure by five days after an ultimatum that initially had a 48-hour window — the kind of escalate/de-escalate cycle that keeps traders glued to their screens.

Mexico's inflation surges to 4.63% in early March, well above forecasts
Mexico's annual inflation accelerated to 4.63% in the first two weeks of March, significantly exceeding Bloomberg's analyst median estimate of 4.37% and the 4.13% recorded at end-February. The data arrives days before Banxico's March 26 rate meeting, where the market is literally split: 15 of 29 analysts expect the rate to hold at 7%, and 14 project a 25 basis-point cut — a rare tie that illustrates just how much uncertainty exists. The board has already had to push back its forecast for inflation to converge on the 3% target to the second quarter of 2027, from the third quarter of 2026.

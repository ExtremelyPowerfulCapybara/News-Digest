CNN Energy
Iran War Pushes Gas to $3.25/Gallon, Diesel Up 11% in a Week — Small Businesses Squeezed
Average U.S. gas prices hit $3.25 a gallon, up 36 cents from last month, while diesel surged 11% week-over-week to $4.16 — a direct consequence of supply disruptions tied to the U.S.-Israel strikes on Iran. Small businesses, already reporting their weakest revenue and hiring expectations since 2020 per a Federal Reserve study released this week, are absorbing the double hit of tariff-driven input costs and now fuel price spikes. For delivery-dependent operators, margin compression is acute and price increases to consumers are increasingly unavoidable.

Bitcoin Short Squeeze Wipes $272M in Positions as BTC Rebounds Above $73K
Bitcoin dropped to the $63,000 zone as Iran-driven geopolitical fear collapsed open interest from ~$29B to ~$21B, flushing leveraged longs. The subsequent stabilization and rebound above $73,000 triggered a sharp short squeeze, with $272M in short positions liquidated and total crypto liquidations hitting $587.97M in 24 hours. Altcoins followed with Solana +9%, Chainlink +7%, and Hyperliquid +12% over seven days — though the Coinbase Premium Index's prior deep negative readings suggest U.S. spot demand remains structurally cautious.

China's 15th Five-Year Plan Bets on Tech Over Consumption Amid Deepening Economic Imbalances
Beijing's new quinquennial plan doubles down on technology as the primary growth engine, explicitly deprioritizing domestic consumption despite well-documented structural imbalances in the Chinese economy. The strategy reflects a political calculation that self-sufficiency in advanced technology — driven in part by U.S. export restrictions — matters more than rebalancing household demand. With the U.S.-China tech race now framed as existential by both sides, the plan accelerates a bifurcation of global technology supply chains that carries long-term implications for multinational earnings and trade flows.

Evervault Raises $25M Series B to Encrypt Payment Data at Point of Collection
Evervault, backed by Ribbit Capital, Sequoia, and Index Ventures, has raised $25M to scale its payment data encryption platform that ensures sensitive card data never exists in plaintext — on any server, including the merchant's. The company uses AWS Nitro Enclaves and a dual-custody key model via Shamir's Secret Sharing, and claims clients can reduce their PCI DSS compliance scope to its simplest tier, saving roughly $100,000 annually. With payment fraud losses running into the tens of billions globally, the infrastructure-layer approach to eliminating plaintext data exposure is gaining traction with regulated financial institutions.

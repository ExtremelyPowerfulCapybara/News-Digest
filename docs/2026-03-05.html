U.S. average gas price posts largest single-day jump in four years
AAA data shows the national average for regular gasoline rose 9 cents overnight, bringing the weekly gain to 22 cents and the current average to $3.20 per gallon. The spike compounds pre-existing pressure from refiners switching to summer blends and represents a direct consumer-facing transmission of the Iran conflict risk premium. GlobeNewswire Markets
Royal Cup to acquire Farmer Brothers for $1.29/share in all-cash deal, taking FARM private
Farmer Brothers (NASDAQ: FARM), a 114-year-old national coffee roaster and distributor, has agreed to be acquired by privately held Royal Cup Coffee & Tea in an all-cash transaction at $1.29 per share, with Braemont Capital providing the strategic capital. The combined entity would represent over 250 years of combined industry history with a national direct-store-delivery network serving independent restaurants through large institutional buyers. The deal is expected to close by June 30, 2026, and follows a pattern of consolidation in the foodservice distribution space as margin pressure squeezes smaller public operators.

Spellbook raises $40M debt facility from RBCx two months after $350M Series B, targeting legal AI acquisitions
Toronto-based Spellbook, which serves nearly 4,000 law firms across 80 countries, secured a $40M USD debt facility from RBC's innovation banking arm to fund acquisitions as the legal AI market consolidates rapidly. Aroundtown reports €288M FFO1 for 2025, resumes dividend after three-year pause as European real estate stabilizes
Aroundtown (ETR: AT1) posted full-year 2025 FFO1 of €288M, down from €316M in 2024, with higher finance expenses of €243M cited as the primary drag from refinancing at elevated rates. The company recorded a €1.13B reported profit — inflated by €459M in deferred tax income tied to a phased reduction in the German corporate tax rate from 15% to 10% by 2032 — and a €239M goodwill impairment that management said did not affect operating KPIs. Finland housing starts set for record low in 2026 as oversupply, high costs, and falling prices stall recovery
Industry group Rakli forecasts only 15,000–17,000 new apartment units will be started in Finland this year, extending a slump that already hit its lowest level since records began in the late 1960s in 2025.
